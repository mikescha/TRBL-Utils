We calculated an Acoustic Reproductive Index (ARI) to summarize offspring acoustic activity relative to pre-hatch female acoustic activity for each site-level breeding pulse. Confirmed recording-level detections were used rather than counts of individual calls. For each pulse with a usable hatch date, we defined a female reference window as 7 calendar days preceding hatch, from hatch −9 through hatch −3, clipped to the deployment period when recordings began after the start of the window. We defined the nestling window as the 7 calendar days from hatch +5 through hatch +11, clipped to the deployment end date when recordings ended before the full window was available. Only recordings from the standardized daytime monitoring period, 07:00 through 19:59, were included. Site/pulse records were considered not ARI-scorable when they lacked a usable hatch date, had fewer than four span days in either the female or nestling analysis window, lacked female detections needed to form the denominator, or represented breeding types considered unsuitable for a single hatch-relative ARI calculation.

For each window, we calculated the number of available recordings and the number of recordings with validated detections of the target call type. Female detection rate was calculated as the number of recordings with validated female detections divided by the total number of recordings in the female reference window. Nestling detection rate was calculated analogously for validated nestling detections in the nestling window. ARI was then calculated as the nestling detection rate divided by the female detection rate. We also summarized fledgling detections in a later hatch-relative window, from hatch +9 through hatch +16, clipped to deployment end. Because ARI is an acoustic index rather than a direct reproductive fate assignment, we report both the continuous ARI value and a descriptive ARI class. ARI classes distinguish records that were not ARI-scorable, records with no detected offspring acoustic evidence, records with reduced offspring acoustic activity, and records with high offspring acoustic activity. These ARI classes are intended to aid interpretation of the acoustic data and are not treated as replacements for manually interpreted reproductive outcome categories.
